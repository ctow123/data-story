The Data Pipeline: from Creation to Visualization

Data Collection

Abstract - The Goal

Data collection at its core is a way for society to achieve expediancy or do things efficiently (Katz). Collection techniques embody this modern compulsion for compression, calculation, and circulation of information. (Noble and Robert) The act of collection itself brings up ethical questions about privacy

Creating Data

Any activity you do, online or offline, creates data that can be captured. The activity I choose to record was my searches to see how I allocate my time. Google already does this practice for searches on its engine

Capturing Data

To collect data, you have to choose what metrics to track. This reveals the ethics and values of a society as data is not collected for fun, but serves a purpose. Oftentimes these metrics can have a profound effect on what trends and groups are represented as metrics have inherent biases that people should be aware of

Data Processing

Abstract - The Goal

Data processing is a process of transformation. The organization and classification pattern used to process the data is also revealing about the ethical code of the collector and society. (Listing Techniques) Processing is done to clean up the data for algorithms and visualization.

Pre-processing

Data sets need to be pre-processed to be run through algorithms and visualized. This involves organizing the data by classifcation of points and potential removal of extraneous values. For my project, I had to group searches into a few big categories to have some overlap in data points

Algorithms

Algorithms are a way of filtering and presenting revelvant data to us. Some problems are transparency as algos are generally blackboxes. This is problematic because we accept algos as presenting the unbiased truth , when algos can have lots of biases. For my project, I used excel formulas to filter out my raw data sheet

Data Visualization

Abstract - The Goal

Visualization shows the purpose the data served, which reflects societal values. (Listing Techniques) The visualizations help circulate the collected and compressed data. Furthur, they help us understand the world that we live in and notice trends perhaps invisible to the eye

The Dangers of Data and Algorithms - Amazon Study

The Problem

Companies collect hundreds of data points on customers unknowningly and feed that data into various algorithms. The end results manifest themselves in the form of recommendations, advertisements, or actions taken based on probablistic events. For example, data and algos in policing have led to crime prevention by prediction

Other Ramifactions

Two big problems are the privacy of the user as data is traded and sold. While mostly harmless individually, all the data points put together poise a bigger threat. The second problem is a lack of transparency in algorithms, which may led to actions based on untrue, manipulated, or bias results due to flaws in the algo (Courtland)

Possible Reguluations

Privacy by accountability. Create a government agency (Federal Data Comission?) that monitors company's data and algorithmic practices for malicious intent. This would give citizens recourse over companies already engaged in shady data practices
